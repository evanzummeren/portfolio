Historical Dutch Asiatic Trading

Historical Dutch-Asiatic Trading (HDAT) shows an interpolation of the historical Dutch-Asiatic and Intra-Asiatic trading lines. The data visualisation, which I made with RJ Korteschiel, shows the overall size of the trade. With the help of minard lines the trading size of individual goods are also shown.

The underlying data is based on the Boekhouders-Generaal Batavia (BGB) database, a database that was digitized and provided by the Huygens ING. The BGB contains data on the departure or arrival of around 20 000 ships in the 18th century, and the inventory of these ships.

A network, based on the atlas Van Keulen and various shipping logs, was drawn in QGIS. Through PgRouting and PostGIS these routes were connected with the ships. The sailing speeds were interpolated with the help of the data from the daily shipping logs.

Frontend was done in Leaflet and React.

Later on I used the database and the visualisation as the base for my bachelor thesis on the Dutch trade with Persia.
